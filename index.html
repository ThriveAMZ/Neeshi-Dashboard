Methodology

Net proceeds figures are pulled directly from Amazon's SKU Economics report. Cost of goods sold is applied per ASIN (configurable in the Files tab) against net units sold (units kept after returns) to produce true profit. Returned units are not re-charged COGS as Amazon's report already accounts for refund admin fees and referral fee refunds in net proceeds. Sponsored Products charges in the SKU Economics file include all SP campaign spend across active and paused targets in the period.

Sponsored Products charges in the P&L pull from the SKU Economics report and may differ slightly from the sum of the Targeting export, which uses a different attribution window. The P&L value is the figure attributed to the SKU's economics for the period.

Targeting metrics are pulled from Amazon's Targeting export. Status flags reflect the target and campaign state at the end of the most recent week of the period. paused means the target itself is paused or archived, or its parent campaign is paused or archived (a paused campaign overrides any target setting beneath it). For targets whose target and campaign are both enabled, activated means there was no spend in the earliest week of the period and there is spend in the most recent week, dormant means no spend in any week of the period, and active means the target is enabled and currently spending.

Data management

Export downloads the current state as data.json. Push this to the client's GitHub repo each week to update their dashboard.

Import loads a previously exported data.json back into this browser, so you can resume work on a client.

Clear wipes this browser's saved data and reloads. If the page is hosted with a data.json, that loads next; otherwise the page returns to its built-in empty state.
